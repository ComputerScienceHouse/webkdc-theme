What is WebLogin?

The Web-based service you are using requires you to identify yourself with your Stanford University Network (SUNet) ID. You must provide your SUNet ID and password to continue to the requested service or Web page. (If you don't have a SUNet ID, see What if I don't have a SUNet ID below.)

Under this system, called Web Authentication, or WebAuth, your single weblogin gives you access to many other Stanford websites besides the one you requested. In essence, you receive two keys when you login: a key to the specific website or service you requested, and a "skeleton" key that opens other protected websites as well. Those sites will just let you in without you logging in again. However, other sites that require weblogin may not recognize the skeleton key, and may require you to do a new weblogin again.

The keys last until you quit your browser program, or until they expire (they can last up to 10 hours from the time of login). Be sure you have "turned in your keys" by quitting your browser before you leave your computer unprotected; otherwise, someone else using your computer after you can get access to the web - both to the sites for which you got specific keys as well as to any of the other Stanford websites that accept the skeleton key.

What am I supposed to do?

On the WebLogin page you came from (use the Back button or command to return to it), enter your SUNet ID and password and click on the Login button. Use the Tab key or your mouse to put the cursor into the entry fields.

If you do not have a SUNet ID and password, or if you don't want to logon to WebAuth, you can click on the "I don't want to logon" link on the WebLogin page. That link may take you to a "public" version or page of the website. If it does not, then the service provides no public access.

SUNet ID: Enter the Login form of your SUNet ID (Stanford University Network Identifier), which is 3 to 8 characters long. If you have a Leland account, the name of this account is the same as your SUNet ID; e.g., if you receive e-mail at "jdoe@stanford.edu", your SUNet ID is "jdoe".

Password: Enter the password associated with your SUNet ID. If you have a Leland account, this password is the same as the password you use to access your Leland account. If you have forgotten your SUNet ID or password, contact Stanford IT Help Desk, phone (650) 725-HELP (725-4357), or submit a HelpSU ticket with HelpSU Online. More information about SUNet IDs is available here.

What if I don't have a SUNet ID?

Most Stanford community members are eligible for a SUNet ID. To get one, or find out more, click here.

If you are not a Stanford community member, you can click on the "I don't want to logon" link on the WebLogin page. That link may take you to a "public" version or page of the website. If it doesn't, then the web service you are requesting may not be available to the public.

Help! What did I do wrong?

Error messages and descriptions:

Missing or Incorrect SUNet ID and/or Password: To login to Stanford WebAuth, you must provide both your SUNet ID and its password. It must be the Login form of your SUNet ID, which is 3 to 8 characters long. If you have forgotten your SUNet ID or password or need other assistance with them, contact Stanford IT Help Desk, phone (650) 725-HELP (725-4357), or submit a HelpSU ticket at HelpSU Online. More information about SUNet IDs can also be found on the SUNet ID home page.

You must enable cookies on your web browser: Stanford WebAuth requires that your web browser accept "cookies", small files that web servers send to your computer. Cookies have many purposes, some of them of questionable value to you. However, WebAuth uses them for security and verification. Having a cookie for a Stanford WebAuthed website identifies you to the website and allows you to continue from one page of the site to another without having to login each time. You can usually enable cookies in the Settings or Preferences panels of your browser program.

You took too long to login: You must enter your SUNet ID and password within 2 minutes of the WebLogin screen appearing. If you don't, you will get this error message. You must re-initiate the request for the web page or service you want - that may mean re-entering the URL or returning to the page with the link to this service. Backing up to the WebLogin page and trying again will not work.

Why doesn't my password work?

Does your password work for email purposes, but not get recognized by Weblogin? That's an indication that your password has not been reset in the past couple years. Most likely, resetting your password will clear up that problem.

There are three options for resetting your password:

  • Use MacLeland or PC-Leland's "Change password...." features to reset it yourself
  • Login to one of the Leland systems (elaine, cardinal, etc.) using Samson and use the "passwd" command to reset it yourself, or
  • Call the Stanford IT Help Desk at 650-725-4357 (725-HELP) and ask them to assist you.
Why does WebLogin look different than before?

These WebLogin pages are part of WebLogin version 3. WebLogin version 3 works very differently from the earlier version 2 (in Weblogin 2, the title says SUNet ID Login, and the picture is an aerial view of the front of the Stanford Quad, with the words "Stanford University" overlaid on the sky). Both versions will co-exist at Stanford for a year or so as websites switch from the old to the new. Because of the differences, weblogins in one version are not recognized in the other, so you may have to login more times than you would expect during the transition.

More information about the two systems is available here.
